# Media Talking Points

## Short positioning

AI trust is no longer only about model safety.

It is about verifying identity, communication, custody, continuity, and proof across systems organizations already depend on.

---

## Key statement

The next wave of AI-enabled fraud is not only attacking passwords.

It is attacking trust.

---

## Platform statement

Platforms like Canvas, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, and other cloud systems provide enormous value.

The problem is not that these systems exist.

The problem is when institutions allow one platform to become the sole custody point for identity, communication, records, workflow, and continuity.

---

## Dependency statement

The deeper issue is institutional dependency.

When too much trust is concentrated inside one vendor-controlled environment, the impact of disruption expands far beyond normal downtime.

---

## AI-speed statement

AI-speed fraud changes the environment because attackers can increasingly imitate tone, timing, familiarity, and institutional context.

The risk is not only stolen data.

The risk is trusted context being reused against organizations.

---

## Trust layer statement

A login only proves entry.

It does not independently prove:

- who acted
- what changed
- what communication was trusted
- what was copied
- what survives after platform failure

Organizations need an independent trust layer beside the platforms they already use.

---

## Core phrase bank

- One login is not enough.
- Trust needs its own layer.
- Keep the platform. Remove sole custody.
- AI fraud is becoming a trust problem.
- The breach is only part of the story. Dependency is the bigger issue.
- Platforms deliver workflow. Trust layers preserve proof.

---

## Closing statement

The future is not simply stronger passwords.

The future is independent trust infrastructure.
