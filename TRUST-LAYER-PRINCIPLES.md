# Trust Layer Principles

## Principle 1 — One login is not enough

Authentication alone does not fully establish trust.

Organizations increasingly need independent verification around identity, communication, continuity, and proof.

---

## Principle 2 — Platforms can deliver workflow without owning all trust

A platform may provide enormous operational value while still creating dangerous dependency concentration.

Organizations should reduce sole custody dependence.

---

## Principle 3 — AI fraud is becoming a trust problem

The next generation of fraud increasingly targets:

- context
- familiarity
- timing
- institutional relationships
- communication confidence
- escalation assumptions

not only passwords.

---

## Principle 4 — Independent proof matters

Organizations should be able to independently verify:

- who acted
- what changed
- what communication was trusted
- what continuity assumptions existed
- what survives platform disruption

---

## Principle 5 — Continuity is part of trust

Operational continuity and institutional trust are connected.

If organizations cannot continue operating clearly during disruption, trust erodes quickly.

---

## Principle 6 — Dependency visibility is necessary

Organizations often underestimate how much trust has accumulated inside vendor-controlled systems.

Dependency mapping is now part of operational governance.

---

## Principle 7 — Keep the platform. Remove sole custody.

The strongest future path is not necessarily abandoning platforms.

The stronger path is reducing dependency concentration while preserving workflow value.
